Frequently Asked Questions

What do you inspect?

The following items are included in every inspection:

  • Interior - walls, ceilings and floors; stairs, balconies and railings; all accessible doors, windows, security bars; cabinets and counters; all accessible ceiling fans, light fixtures, electrical outlets and smoke detectors; door bell; self-lighting fireplaces
  • Kitchen - dishwasher, garbage disposal, microwave, exhaust vent, cook top/oven/range, sink & visible plumbing, GFCIs, refrigerator/freezer
  • Bathrooms - sinks & visible plumbing, mirrors, exhaust fans, bath tubs & showers, shower walls and enclosure, toilets
  • Laundry - visible plumbing, washer & dryer, exhaust fan, gas valve
  • Garage - garage door, garage door opener & reverse status, GFCI
  • Exterior structure - paint, stucco & siding condition; roof condition
  • Grounds - driveway, walkway & patio condition; vegetation and grading condition;
  • Plumbing - all interior and exterior faucets and fixtures; water pressure reading from exterior faucet; water heater; visible exterior plumbing; water meter
  • Utilities - main gas valve, electrical panel (inside and out)

What is NOT covered in an inspection?

The following items will NOT be inspected:

  • Any walls, floors, windows, outlets, appliances, cabinets, counters, etc. obstructed from view by furniture, boxes, stored personal items, etc. The inspector will NOT move ANY personal items during the inspection. This may also include washing machines/dryers containing clothing, ovens or dishwashers with stored dishes, or other items.
  • Elevators, lifts or dumbwaiters; built-in vacuum systems and any components installed or modified after original construction of the building
  • Security systems; fire & life-safety systems; fire-sprinklers, music & intercoms; low-voltage systems of any kind
  • Thermostats, time clocks (includes irrigation controllers) or photoelectric controls or components
  • Water softeners, water filtration systems (spa, pool or culinary), water or air purification or treatment systems, humidifiers
  • Landscape irrigation systems and their components including valves, distribution piping, anti-siphon devices, timers and control systems
  • The condition and/or irrigation of trees, shrubs or vegetation of any kind, except as stated in NAC 645d.580.2(c)
  • Any system or component which is not accessible for visual inspection, is hidden from view, impractical or dangerous to inspect including certain attics or crawl spaces per NAC 645d.470(5); Any item or component not safely reachable or inspectable with a 12’ ladder, per NAC 645d.060.
  • Any system or component not specifically listed in the Nevada “Standards of Practice” (NAC 645d.480-.580) as an inspection requirement.
  • Any system, component, fixture or condition noted in the inspection report as ‘not inspected’ or ‘not inspectable,’ ‘inaccessible,’ ‘visual-access-restricted,’ ‘not determined,’ ‘not reported on’ or similar language, such as light fixtures without light bulbs, etc.
  • Environmental hazards, materials, substances or conditions including, but not limited to; any type of Fungus and Mold; toxic, reactive, combustible, corrosive or otherwise dangerous contaminants; wildfire; odors; noise or noisy components; Flood-Plain rating; electromagnetic fields; underground storage tanks; asbestos; radon gas; lead paint; UREA formaldehyde, PCB’s; water or air quality, the proximity to toxic waste sites; other environmental or health hazards
